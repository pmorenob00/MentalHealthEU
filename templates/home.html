Welcome to Mental Health in the EU

  1. Home

Mental health is an essential pillar for the well-being of individuals and communities. On this platform, we provide a clear and accessible overview of the state of mental health in European Union countries, based on the results of two key surveys conducted in 2023 and 2014.

Our goal is to present this data in an understandable and visually appealing way, making it easy to interpret and analyse. Here you will find interactive graphics, infographics and summaries that highlight the main trends and conclusions derived from the surveys. These resources will allow you to quickly and effectively understand the mental health situation in the region, its challenges and progress over time.

This site is designed to be a useful tool for academics, health professionals, policy makers and anyone interested in the topic. Discover the data, explore our visualisations and get an informed perspective on mental health in the European Union.

Welcome to a space where information is transformed into accessible and valuable knowledge!

Questionare

This section shows the responses collected from both questionnaires to put us in context.

The 2014 questionnaire presents a series of questions about what physical and emotional problems respondents have experienced in the last 4 weeks and how they have affected their daily activities.

The 2023 questionnaire addresses the same point as the 2014 questionnaire but all together in the first question, then addresses different themes and hot topics in Europe.

Here you can get an idea of the current state of mental and physical health of EU citizens, how it affects their lives and how they deal with this problem.

Comparative

This section is dedicated to comparing data where questions overlap, either by year or by social group.

The comparison by year 2023 vs 2014 compares the number of people who went to a mental health professional when they had a mental health issue.

The comparison of mental health patients vs other patients shows how people perceive patients according to whether they have a mental health problem or a physical problem, depending on their social group in 2023.

Here you can look at data whose questions match to compare how it has evolved over the years or the differences depending on the social group you belong to.

Mental Health Stigma

This section is dedicated to European society's perception of people with mental health issues and how this affects their lives and how they are treated by others.

The general section deals with the data in a more generic way without going into detail.

The specific section deals with the same questions but with more detail and a wider range of answers.

Here you can get an idea of how mental health problems can affect your life and what others think of you.

War
